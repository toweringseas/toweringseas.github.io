Climate Control

Climate control at the beach is its own little science! Our proximity to the water and the prevailing winds mean that our bungalow can get humid from time to time. So we have a mix of air conditioners and dehumidifiers across the property.

Each of the living spaces has an air conditioning unit with a remote control. In the primary bedroom, the remote control is on the bedside table. In the living room, the remote control is attached by magnet to the refrigerator. In the game room, the remote control is on the table by the air conditioning unit.

We suggest keeping the temperature at 70° in each of those living spaces. Please keep the mode set to auto. Setting the temperature lower than 68° will result in condensation on the floorboards. We usually shut down the air conditioner in the primary bedroom overnight if it’s too noisy.

If you need additional humidity control, you can turn on the dehumidifier located in the primary bedroom. Simply press the on/off button as the other settings are set optimally. Water will be pumped out of the house so there’s no need to dump the water canister. Before turning the unit on, please make sure the gray tube is securely attached to the back of the dehumidifier.

Local Attractions and Activities

Beaches

You can access the beach by walking down the deck to Ocean Drive and heading left (to the 600 block access) or right (to the 700 block access). They’re approximately the same distance. If you have a beach cart, we suggest using the 600 block access since there are no stairs.

Beach regulations:

  • All beach equipment, including tents, canopies, and volleyball nets, must be removed each night. Equipment left overnight will be confiscated.
  • Overnight camping is not permitted on the beach.
  • Dogs are allowed on the beach but must be leashed at all times. Owners must clean up after their pets.
  • Fires are not allowed on the beach, and fireworks that leave the ground or explode are illegal.
  • Climbing on the dunes is prohibited to protect these natural barriers from erosion.
  • Littering is prohibited, and visitors should use the trash and recycling containers provided.
  • Driving on the beach is permitted for four-wheel-drive vehicles with a town-issued permit from September 15 to April 30 (excluding Easter week). Access ramps are located at specific points along the beach.
  • Holes deeper than 12 inches must be filled before leaving the beach to prevent hazards for others and for sea turtles.

Beach Warning Flag System

  • Yellow Flag: Moderate hazards; use caution while swimming.
  • Red Flag: High hazard; dangerous rip currents, stay out of the water.
  • Double Red Flags: Water is closed to swimming.
  • Purple Flag: Presence of hazardous marine life like jellyfish.
